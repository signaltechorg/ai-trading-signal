## The question

Phase 4's regime-routed gate failed: the current entries (classic momentum, vwap-ema-bb mean-reversion) had no per-regime edge after ~0.4% round-trip crypto-perp costs on H1. Phase 4.5 asked: does ANY single-asset entry strategy on OHLCV data clear those costs? Answered with three studies built on the Phase 4 costed harness.

---

## The three studies

### 1 — Cost-focused literature research

The governing law: viability ∝ (edge per trade) / (cost per trade). Cost is fixed at ~0.4% round-trip regardless of timeframe. The only levers are a bigger move per trade (higher TF, wider targets) and fewer trades.

Findings:
- Breakeven cost for intraday crypto momentum is 3–10 bps. We pay ~40 bps. H1 single-asset timing is structurally foreclosed, not a tuning problem.
- The one documented timing edge surviving realistic costs is daily time-series momentum (~28-day lookback, ~5-day hold, Sharpe ~1.5 at 15 bps). Source: Han/Kang/Ryu 2023 — TS momentum strong, cross-sectional weak after costs.
- Cross-sectional momentum is weak and fragile out-of-sample.
- Carry/funding harvesting is the strongest raw crypto edge (Sharpe 6+ in-sample, decaying into 2025). It is NOT a timing signal and needs funding-rate data we do not have.
- Target widths below ~2% gross are structurally unviable. Need 4–8% multi-day moves.

### 2 — Empirical edge-map (108 cells, Phase 4 costed harness)

Three symbols × three timeframes × three entries × geometry sweep. Deterministic. Crypto perp costs throughout.

Only trustworthy positive cost-adjusted cell: **classic momentum + wide 4R targets on H1** — BTC +4.50%/n143/PF1.15, ETH +2.93%/n78/PF1.12 (SOL break-even). That is +0.03–0.05%/trade after costs — real but marginal.

Every entry at the live 2R geometry is negative with a trustworthy sample, on every timeframe. Higher TF (H4/D1) did NOT concentrate edge — raw edge collapsed as friction shrank, and every D1 cell was thin (729 daily bars over 2 years). Critical caveat: the map ran the existing `classic` scorer on D1 bars — NOT a proper daily time-series momentum filter. The research's #1 candidate was never tested in the map, and the 2-year D1 window was too short to test it anyway.

### 3 — Deep-daily validation: daily time-series momentum on 10 majors × ~2100 bars

The research's #1 candidate, tested faithfully for the first time. Experiment: `docs/research/experiments/daily-momentum-validation-BTCUSD_ETHUSD_SOLUSD_BNBUSD_XRPUSD_ADAUSD_DOGEUSD_DOTUSD_LINKUSD_AVAXUSD-D1-f4.json`, REGISTRY 2026-06-12.

Spec: 28-day TS momentum (price above/below 28-day SMA), signal-flip exit (ride the trend to the opposite cross), 10 liquid majors, ~2090–2190 daily bars each (2020–2026), four contiguous folds. Crypto perp costs throughout. No parameters tuned.

Deployable bar: mean costed return AND expectancy > 0, ≥ 6/10 symbols positive-and-adequate (≥ 30 trades each), fold stability > 50%.

Results:

| Config | Verdict | Mean costed return | Ex-flukes mean | Symbols positive/adequate | Fold stability |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| signal-flip | MARGINAL | +24.92% | **−5.25%** | 4/10 | 38% (32/40 fold cells thin) |
| geometry-2R | NEGATIVE | −12.54% | −12.54% | 0/10 | 30% (all 40 fold cells thin) |
| geometry-4R | NEGATIVE | −14.01% | −14.01% | 0/10 | 30% (all 40 fold cells thin) |

Signal-flip headline is inflated by two single-asset flukes: SOLUSD +189% and AVAXUSD +102%, both driven by the 2020–21 launch-era bull run. Strip those two and the cross-symbol mean is **−5.25%**. Six of the other eight majors are negative. Four of ten positive (below the 6/10 majority bar). Fold stability 38% — 32 of 40 fold cells are thin (< 30 trades), so the stability read itself is underpowered.

Geometry 2R and 4R are flatly negative across every measure. Daily bars are too infrequent for geometry exits to accumulate adequate samples at this lookback length.

**No config clears the deployable bar.**

---

## The conclusion

Single-asset OHLCV timing — momentum or mean-reversion, on H1/H4/D1, even with wide targets, signal-flip trend-riding, and selectivity — is marginal-at-best after ~0.4% crypto costs. Strip the two launch-era flukes and the typical major loses money.

The track-record-as-moat thesis cannot be built on this. A forward track record on these methods would be flat-to-negative and would not survive due diligence. This is not an engineering failure. The engine is correct; it can only route and measure edge that exists, and the edge is not there in single-asset timing.

## The durable asset

Phases 3–4.5 produced a validated edge-vs-noise test bench: the regime engine, the costed regime-conditioned and flip-exit backtest harness, honest strategy attribution, the shadow recorder, and the daily-momentum module. Its value is precisely that it kills bad strategies before a customer sees them — exactly what a track-record business needs. The machinery is reusable for the next edge candidate.

## Strategic options (ranked)

### 1 — Carry / funding-rate harvesting (recommended primary direction)

The strongest documented crypto edge. NOT a timing signal — this is delta-neutral perp basis harvesting, a structural yield. Needs funding-rate data (free via Binance fapi/v1/fundingRate) and a carry engine. This is a data-acquisition plus new-engine decision, not an entry parameter tweak.

Caveat: in-sample Sharpe 6+ is decaying hard into 2025. Do not assume it forward. Validate with a costed harness run on historical funding rates before committing engineering time.

### 2 — Cross-sectional momentum (secondary, cheap to test)

Short-horizon, 2-week, weekly rotation across the ~30-symbol universe. Buildable from existing OHLCV — no new data sources. The existing harness now supports the pieces. Out-of-sample fragile per the literature; worth a cheap harness test before committing further.

### 3 — Reposition to daily trend-regime context (honest, lower ceiling)

Sell the regime engine's market-context output rather than "winning entry signals." Honest and defensible. Lower revenue ceiling — the product becomes an analytical tool, not an execution signal.

### 4 — Accept the marginal classic+4R-H1 cell and shadow-monitor it (low conviction)

Only BTC/ETH, +0.04%/trade, fragile. Single-regime, single-geometry artifact at the margin. Not a foundation for a track-record product.

---

## Recommendation

Pursue carry as the primary edge-bearing pivot. This requires a data-acquisition decision (historical + live Binance funding rates) before any engineering begins. Run a cheap cross-sectional harness test as a secondary while the carry data is being acquired. Do NOT ship single-asset timing signals as if they have edge.

The product narrative must shift: away from "we have winning entry signals" (not supported by the evidence) and toward either a carry-based structural edge or an honest market-context framing.

Phase 5 (execution/webhook go-live) stays gated on edge that does not yet exist.
